★ THE LORE ★

01

EL BEBÉ — The Origin

It started as a dismissive taunt from rival fans. "El Bebé" — calling the young phenom a baby. But Mbappé embraced it, turning the insult into a symbol of his unshakable confidence. Like any good dictator, he took the mockery and weaponized it. The nickname now adorns banners across stadiums worldwide.

02

THE KEBAB OF DISCORD

After a match, Mbappé was spotted enjoying a kebab. A simple meal. But the internet exploded. "Dictators don't eat kebabs!" screamed the memes. The photo went viral, spawning thousands of edits — Mbappé on a throne eating a kebab, Mbappé signing decrees with a kebab, Mbappé declaring kebab the official state food. The people rejoiced.

03

CLUB TAKEOVER

Whispers became shouts: Mbappé didn't just play for PSG — he ran PSG. Transfer decisions, tactical choices, who gets the armband. The "dictator" label stuck. When he signed that monstrous contract, complete with image rights and a say in sporting direction, the memes wrote themselves. "El Presidente" had assumed control.

04

TRAINING GROUND TAKEOVER

Stories emerged of Mbappé running training sessions himself, demanding specific drills, and once allegedly rearranging the cones in a formation only he approved of. Teammates either love him or fear him. Probably both. The training ground became his first conquered territory.

05

PRESS CONFERENCE DEMANDS

Reporters learned quickly: you don't ask Mbappé certain questions. The press room has new rules. No questions about his future. No questions about the manager. No questions about "that" kebab. The dictator sets the agenda. A golden podium now sits permanently at his designated spot.

06

THE HANDSHAKE INCIDENT

The moment that cemented the meme. A teammate offered a handshake. Mbappé stared. Paused. Walked past. The footage looped for days. "That's how dictators greet people," the internet decided. The cold shoulder heard 'round the world. Now fans recreate the snub in every stadium.
